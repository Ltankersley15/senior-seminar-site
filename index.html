Senior Seminar Research

The Effect of Multidirectional Fiber Mats and Stitching Density on the Stress Distribution in Stitched Carbon Fiber Composites

This research will test wet-stitch carbon laminates with various layer types provided by Yonsei University using 3-point bending & tension with 3D DIC, model composites with Abaqus, quantify dynamic behavior via laser vibrometry, and optionally design dry-stitched, resin-infused panels.

Fall 2025 Advisors: Dr. Han-Gyu Kim • Dr. Taehee Lee Team: Lonnie Tankersley, Garv Patel, Christopher Bolton, Ethan White
See Latest Work

Project Definition

Stitched composites are increasingly used for lightweight, durable structures. Yonsei-manufactured, wet-stitched pre-preg laminates provide a chance to study interlaminar damage propagation, stiffness degradation, and modal behavior relevant to aerospace and infrastructure applications.

Problem Statement

The study aims to compare the effect of intermittently placed multidirectional fiber mats as well as the through-thickness, square stitching impact on panel delamination and rigidity.

Impact

Results inform stitched-composite design for primary structures and composite-reinforced infrastructure.

Partners & Facilities

  • Yonsei University — specimens
  • Mississippi State ACI — 3D DIC, Shimadzu UTM, resin infusion
  • Aerostructures for Hypersonic Flight Research Lab (Walker 123) — laser vibrometry

Scope of Work

Semester objectives and methods aligned to deliver a validated dataset and presentation-ready results.

Objectives

  • Measure & speckle all Yonsei specimens.
  • Run three-point bending & tension tests.
  • Use 3D DIC for full-field strain maps (at ACI).
  • Develop & validate an Abaqus model.
  • Laser vibrometer modal testing (fn, ζ, mode shapes) at Walker 123.
  • Deliver a clean dataset for Yonsei; prepare AIAA-quality visuals.
  • Determine effect of multidirectional fiber layers.
  • Stretch goal: design & manufacture dry-stitched, resin-infused panels at ACI.

Methods

Specimen prep & QA; DIC calibration (ACI); static tests with synchronized imaging; numerical correlation in Abaqus; vibro-modal excitation & ID at Walker 123; compare stitched architecture effects.

Reporting

  • Bi-weekly / monthly meetings with Dr. Han-Gyu Kim.
  • Email summaries & data to Dr. Taehee Lee as needed.
  • Ongoing website documentation & Gantt updates.
  • Formal class updates and end-of-term report.

Success Criteria

  • Calibrated DIC & clean strain maps (ACI).
  • Repeatable load–displacement curves.
  • Abaqus modeling within agreed tolerances.
  • Identified modal shifts vs. stitch architecture (Walker 123).
